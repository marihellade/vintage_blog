This book wrecked me in the best possible way. Thomil and Sciona are two of the most compelling characters I've read in years — a brilliant woman fighting for recognition in a system that was never built for her, and a man from the margins who sees the rot at the centre of everything.

The magic system is fascinating, genuinely original. But it's the ethical weight of it that makes this book unforgettable. Every discovery peels back another layer of something awful, and you can't look away.

I put it down and just sat there for a while.
