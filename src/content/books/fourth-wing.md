There's something about a book that throws you into a war college on the back of a dragon and just says *good luck*. Fourth Wing grabbed me by the collar and didn't let go. Violet Sorrengail is the kind of protagonist I want to root for — not because she's powerful from page one, but because she's stubborn enough to survive what should have killed her ten times over.

The world-building is lush in all the right places. The politics feel real without drowning you in lore. And Xaden... well. If you know, you know.

I tore through this in two days and immediately needed the next one.
