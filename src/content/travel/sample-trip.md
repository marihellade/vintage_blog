Sometimes you just need to stand at the edge of something vast and remember you're small. The Blue Mountains did that for me. We drove up on Friday evening, checked into a creaky old guesthouse that smelled like woodsmoke and lavender, and I didn't look at my phone for two days.

The trails in the mist felt like walking into another world — the kind of forest that makes you understand why people used to believe in forest spirits. Eucalyptus everywhere, dripping and silver.

Found a cafe tucked into a side street that had been there for decades. Scones with thick cream and jam. A fire going even though it was barely cold enough. The kind of place you want to keep secret and also tell everyone about.
