This is the recipe I come back to every few weeks. The dough is forgiving and the result always looks more impressive than the effort involved.

## My notes

- Use good olive oil — you can taste it. Don't skimp.
- I let it rise overnight in the fridge for better flavour. Worth the wait.
- Dimple the dough aggressively. More dimples = more crispy olive oil pockets.
- Fresh rosemary from the garden, always. Dried doesn't come close.
- I added roasted garlic cloves last time and it was *incredible*.
- Flaky salt on top, not fine salt. The crunch matters.

## The basic method

1. Mix flour, water, yeast, salt, and a generous glug of olive oil
2. Let it rise until doubled (or overnight in the fridge)
3. Stretch into an oiled pan, dimple with your fingers
4. Top with olive oil, rosemary, flaky salt
5. Bake at 220°C until golden and fragrant
6. Try not to eat it all in one sitting (you will fail)
