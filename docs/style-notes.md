# Style Evolution Notes

A running log of aesthetic decisions, influences, and changes.

---

## 2026-03-28 — Initial style direction established

**Influences:**
- Abigail Larson — dark fairy tale illustration, gothic elegance
- Gretel Lusky — watercolour fantasy, soft but moody
- Kay Nielsen — art nouveau, golden age illustration, ornate silhouettes
- Magdalina Dianova — detailed fairy tale art, nature and mythology

**What was extracted:**
- Art nouveau sensibility: elegant linework, botanical motifs, literary feeling
- Earthy/ethereal mood, not bubbly or cute
- Parchment light base — like aged paper in a well-loved book
- Color palette: sage green, forest green, terracotta, muted gold, cream
- Typography direction: Cormorant Garamond for headings (old-world serif), Crimson Text for body (warm, readable)
- Decoration: botanical accents (❧), gold dividers, dotted borders — restrained, not maximalist
- Custom cursor: small botanical sprig that follows the mouse

**Feeling the site should evoke:**
- A beautifully kept grimoire or field journal
- An apothecary that also sells rare books and dried flowers
- Fairy tale darkness without being gloomy
- Feminine and delicate without being girly or sparkly
- Dark academia warmth (not dark academia severity)

**What to avoid:**
- Maximalist clutter — readability is non-negotiable
- Generic fantasy-store branding
- Sparkle/kawaii/cute energy
- Anything that makes it feel like a product or platform

**Rules that emerged:**
- Always prioritise readability over decoration
- Botanical motifs over geometric ones
- Gold as a detail colour, never a primary
- Text contrast must be comfortable for long reading sessions
- Mobile should be clean and simple, desktop can have more atmosphere
